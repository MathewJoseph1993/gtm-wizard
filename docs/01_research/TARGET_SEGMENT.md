# Target Segment Analysis

**Source:** GTM Wizard Research Project
**Confidence Level:** VERY HIGH (95%+)

---

## Primary Segment: Solo Technical Founders with Early Traction

### Definition

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Stage** | $1-5K MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) |
| **Team** | Solo or 1-2 co-founders |
| **Technical** | Can build the product themselves |
| **GTM Approach** | DIY (no sales team) |
| **Funding** | Bootstrapped or very early pre-seed |

### Why This Segment?

**Counter-intuitive finding:** Pre-revenue has 4-5x larger TAM, but Early Traction wins on economics.

| Factor | Pre-Revenue | Early Traction ($1-5K) | Winner |
|--------|-------------|------------------------|--------|
| TAM | 300-400K | 50-100K | Pre-Revenue |
| WTP | $50/mo | **$250/mo** | **5x higher** |
| CAC Payback | 10-11 months | **2-5 months** | **3x faster** |
| Churn by week 3 | 73% | 25% | **3x better** |
| Can diagnose lessons | NO | **YES** | **Can improve** |

**Key insight:** "Payback speed trumps TAM size."

Starting with Early Traction reaches $1M ARR **6+ months earlier** than starting with Pre-Revenue.

### Segment Characteristics

#### Demographics
- Age: 25-45 (typically)
- Location: Global (English-speaking focus)
- Technical background: Engineers, developers, product builders
- Business type: B2B SaaS

#### Psychographics
- **Identity:** Builder first, reluctant seller
- **Motivation:** Prove that product can scale
- **Fear:** Wasting time on wrong GTM approach
- **Desire:** Confidence that they're on the right track

#### Current Behavior
- Spending ~$100/mo on GTM tools (Apollo, Instantly, etc.)
- Sending cold emails but unsure if approach is working
- Participating in Indie Hackers, r/SaaS, Twitter/X founder communities
- Building in public, sharing journey

## Secondary Segments (Expansion Path)

### Expansion Timeline

```
Month 0-3:   BEACHHEAD → Early Traction ($1-5K MRR)
             └─ Land 20-30 customers, prove value

Month 4-6:   EXPAND DOWN → Pre-Revenue ($0 MRR)
             └─ Leverage case studies from Early Traction
             └─ Accept higher churn

Month 7-9:   EXPAND UP → Growing ($5-20K MRR)
             └─ Natural upgrade path
             └─ New pain: time-stress, hiring

Month 10-12: ENTERPRISE MOTION → Seed-Funded
             └─ Position as "proven with 200+ bootstrapped"
```

### Segment Details

#### Pre-Revenue (Expansion at Month 4-6)

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| TAM | 300-400K |
| WTP | $50-100/mo |
| Key Pain | "Does my idea even work?" |
| Positioning | "Prove your idea works" |
| Expected churn | HIGH (73% by week 3) |

**Why expand here:**
- Large TAM for growth
- Early Traction success stories de-risk
- Lower price tier ($199/mo)

#### Growing ($5-20K MRR) (Expansion at Month 7-9)

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| TAM | 30-50K |
| WTP | $300-500/mo |
| Key Pain | Time-stress, hiring decisions |
| Positioning | "Scale without burning out" |
| Expected churn | LOW |

**Why expand here:**
- Natural upgrade from Early Traction
- Higher WTP, lower churn
- New features: team collaboration, advanced analytics

#### Seed-Funded (Expansion at Month 10-12)

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| TAM | 20-30K |
| WTP | $1,000-3,000/mo |
| Key Pain | Velocity, board reporting |
| Positioning | "Proven with 200+ bootstrapped founders" |
| Expected churn | VERY LOW |

**Why expand here:**
- 10x WTP vs bootstrapped
- Credibility from bootstrapped success stories
- Different GTM: accelerators, VC networks
